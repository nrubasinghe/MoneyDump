# Your Money or Your Life
**Authors:** Vicki Robin & Joe Dominguez  
**Category:** Personal Finance / Expense Management  
**RAG Source ID:** book_001

---

## Overview

"Your Money or Your Life" is a transformative personal finance book that reframes money as a representation of life energy — the hours of your life you trade for it. The core philosophy is to align your spending with your values and achieve financial independence through mindful tracking and intentional living.

---

## Core Concept: Life Energy

- **Life Energy** = The time and vitality you exchange for money
- Formula: `Real Hourly Wage = Monthly Income / Total Hours Spent Working (including commute, prep, decompression)`
- Every purchase should be evaluated: "Is this worth X hours of my life?"
- This reframing changes how people perceive and control their expenses

---

## The 9-Step Program

### Step 1: Making Peace with the Past
- Calculate your **lifetime earnings** — every dollar ever earned
- Calculate your **net worth** — what you have to show for it
- Creates awareness without judgment

### Step 2: Being in the Present — Tracking Your Life Energy
- Track **every cent** that comes in and goes out
- No categorization yet — just raw awareness
- Tools: notebook, spreadsheet, or app

### Step 3: Monthly Tabulation
- At month end, total all income and expenses
- Convert dollars to **hours of life energy**
- Example: If real hourly wage = $15, a $300 expense = 20 hours of life

### Step 4: Three Questions
For every expense category ask:
1. Did I receive **fulfillment** proportional to the money spent?
2. Is this expense in **alignment with my values**?
3. How would this change if I didn't **need** the job?

Rate each category: ➕ (want more), ➖ (want less), ✅ (just right)

### Step 5: Making Life Energy Visible — The Wall Chart
- Create a large wall chart with two lines:
  - **Monthly Income** (in hours of life energy)
  - **Monthly Expenses** (in hours of life energy)
- Visual accountability tool
- Goal: widen the gap between income and expenses

### Step 6: Valuing Your Life Energy — Minimizing Spending
- Reduce spending in categories marked ➖
- Ask: What's the minimum I need to be truly satisfied?
- Distinguish between **needs**, **wants**, and **desires**

### Step 7: Valuing Your Life Energy — Maximizing Income
- Increase income without increasing hours
- Side income, passive income, monetizing skills
- Every extra dollar = steps toward financial independence

### Step 8: Capital and the Crossover Point
- **Crossover Point**: When monthly investment income ≥ monthly expenses
- This is **Financial Independence (FI)**
- Formula: `Monthly Investment Income = Capital × Safe Withdrawal Rate`

### Step 9: Managing Your Finances
- Invest capital in safe, income-generating assets
- Treasury bonds, index funds, dividend stocks
- Live off passive income perpetually

## Key Financial Concepts

### Fulfillment Curve
```
Fulfillment
     |          *** Peak (Enough)
     |        *     *
     |      *         *
     |    *             * (Overconsumption)
     |  *
     |*
     +-------------------------> Spending
```
- More spending does NOT equal more happiness beyond "Enough"
- Identify your personal "Enough" point

### Gazingus Pin Concept
- A "gazingus pin" = any item you mindlessly buy when you see it (shoes, gadgets, snacks)
- Identifying these reveals unconscious spending triggers
- Key insight for expense categorization in apps

### The Enough Point
- Not about deprivation — about sufficiency
- "Enough" is the peak of the fulfillment curve
- Beyond enough = clutter, debt, stress

## Practical Tools & Techniques

### Daily Expense Tracking
- Record every transaction same day
- Use categories that feel natural to you
- Review weekly for patterns

### Monthly Review Ritual
1. Total all income
2. Total all expenses by category
3. Convert to life energy hours
4. Apply the 3 questions
5. Update wall chart

### Reducing Expenses Without Deprivation
- **Meal planning** reduces food waste and dining costs
- **Library usage** instead of buying books
- **Delayed gratification** — wait 30 days before non-essential purchases
- **DIY skills** — cooking, repairs, personal care
- **Secondhand shopping** — quality items at lower life energy cost

## Financial Independence Formula

```
Monthly Expenses = $X
Safe Withdrawal Rate = 4%
Capital Needed = X × 12 / 0.04 = X × 300

Example:
Monthly expenses = $2,500
Capital needed = $2,500 × 300 = $750,000
```
